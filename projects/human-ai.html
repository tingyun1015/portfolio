Background

Human-AI Interaction (HAII) Research Proposal

During the module "Seminar on Human-Robot Interaction and Human-AI Interaction: Theories and Research" at NCCU, I explored theoretical frameworks and empirical studies covering key topics such as anthropomorphism, social robotics, and AI ethics. Building upon this academic foundation, I engineered a simulated command-line AI agent to investigate AI-mediated emotional interaction and user behaviour. A subsequent pilot user study revealed that engaging with an emotionally responsive command-line agent significantly increased users' sense of achievement and emotional engagement during complex tasks.

Research Title

TermiGenie - Leveraging an AI Agent to Ease Tech-Related Tensions

Abstract

Interacting with digital systems can often become a source of stress and anxiety for users. Frustration derived from these interactions can significantly reduce their willingness to leverage tools that should otherwise be helpful. Because LLM-powered AI agents can communicate through natural language and act as digital experts, this research explores the opportunity to use them as empathetic mediators between struggling users and rigid digital interfaces.

Focusing on the command-line terminal as one of the most fundamental computing interfaces, I proposed TermiGenie, an AI agent designed to provide conversational explanations for complex terminal outputs. The primary goal was to improve the user's interaction experience solely through these conversational enhancements. To evaluate this, I adopted a within-subjects experimental design to examine whether a purposefully designed AI agent could effectively offer emotional support during technical workflows.

Pilot Study Insights

The results of the within-subjects study revealed that this purposefully designed AI agent effectively provided emotional support during difficult digital interactions:

  • Reduced Anxiety: Users felt significantly less anxious when supported by TermiGenie, experiencing a 57% reduction in negative emotions.
  • Higher Sense of Achievement: Compared to performing tasks without assistance, users reported a noticeably higher sense of accomplishment upon task completion.
  • Increased Engagement: Users experienced greater overall pleasure and emotional engagement during their interaction with the terminal.

These findings strongly suggest that small, supportive AI agents like TermiGenie can act as vital mediators, contributing to a more harmonious and emotionally supportive digital landscape.
